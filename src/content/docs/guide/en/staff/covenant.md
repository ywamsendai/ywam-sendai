Welcome to the Sendai family. Being on staff here is more than a job; it is a "Rule of Life"—a shared rhythm where we seek **Union with God (Theosis)** while being His hands and feet in Japan.

## 1. Our Shared Vision: A Mission Hub for Tohoku
We aren't just a base; we are a "Mission Hub." We believe God called YWAM Sendai to be a healthy, vibrant training center that multiplies ministries across the six prefectures of Tohoku. 
* **Our Goal:** To see Japanese-English bilingual schools that equip people to reach Japan through practical, incarnational ministry.

## 2. Living the YWAM DNA
As a DTS graduate, you carry the "family traits" of YWAM. We ask all staff to:
* **Embrace the Values:** Regularly refresh yourself on YWAM’s [Foundational Values](/vision/values) and [Covenants](/vision/covenants).
* **Deepen the Interior Life:** Invest in your personal relationship with God through daily habits of prayer, Scripture, and worship. We believe our outward mission only has power if it flows from an inward life with Jesus.

## 3. The Sacredness of the Person
We believe every person is a temple of the Holy Spirit. We uphold a "Holistic Sanctity" that honors God with our whole selves:
* **Integrity of the Body:** We honor the created order of male and female and the biblical boundary of marriage (one man and one woman). Living in harmony with our biological sex and biblical sexuality is a key part of our growth in Christ.
* **A Rhythm of Self-Control:** We encourage a lifestyle of "Gentle Asceticism"—using tools like fasting, digital rest, and sobriety to keep our spirits clear and our bodies healthy for long-term service.

:::tip[Longevity Note]
We value your health! As part of our commitment to the "Temple of the Spirit," we encourage staff to pursue healthy sleep, nutrition, and movement.
:::

## 4. The Heart for Japan: Language & Culture
To truly love our neighbors, we must speak their heart language. 
* **The Goal:** Fluency (JLPT N2).
* **The Strategy:** Immersion in Japanese churches, social groups, and media.
* **The Commitment:** * **Years 1–2:** Up to 16 hours/week (50% of ministry time) for study.
    * **Year 3+:** Up to 8 hours/week (25% of ministry time) until N2 is passed.
* **The Language of the House:** We challenge each other to speak Japanese together as our "common language" rather than defaulting to English.

## 5. A Covenant of Presence: Time & Meetings
Our life together is built on showing up for one another.
* **The Weekly Rhythm:** 40 hours per week (80% Ministry / 20% Base Functioning).
* **Core Meetings:** Attendance at all [Core Meetings](/rhythms/meetings) is the glue that holds us together.
* **Digital Connection:** Since we are a bilingual, mobile team, we use **Discord** for "over-communication." Being active online is part of our commitment to one another.

:::note[For Families]
We love our families! Parents of small children are encouraged to share their work hours. We ask that at least one parent attend each core meeting so no one becomes disconnected.
:::

## 6. Financial Stewardship
In the YWAM tradition, we are a "supported" movement.
* **Self-Responsibility:** Staff are responsible for all personal expenses, insurance, and taxes.
* **The Staff Contribution:** Each staff member contributes **¥6,000/month** to the base. This fund supports our national office, building maintenance, hospitality, and disaster relief.
* **State Obligations:** As residents of Japan, you are required by law to enroll in the **National Health Insurance** and **National Pension** systems.

## 7. Rest and Renewal (Leave)
Rest is a spiritual discipline. We provide:
* **Annual Leave:** 20 days per year.
* **Fundraising (Furlough):** Up to 3 weeks/year to visit your support network.
* **Sabbatical:** After 5 years, you can request 3 months for deep spiritual renewal.
