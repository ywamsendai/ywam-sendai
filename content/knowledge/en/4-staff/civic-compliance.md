## 🏛️ Self-Managed Civic Compliance
Because YWAM Sendai staff operate as self-supported volunteers rather than salaried employees, the base cannot execute corporate payroll tax withholding. Staff members are personally and legally responsible for self-managing their own administrative registrations, address updates, banking setups, and civic compliance obligations within the Japanese municipal system.

## 🇯🇵 Mandatory Japanese Tax Filing
All long-term staff residing in Japan are required to file annual tax returns directly with the local Japanese tax office (*Zeimusho*). You must never assume that receiving your financial support via overseas donations, filing taxes in your home country, or operating under a missionary status exempts you from your legal duty to report financial income inside Japan.

## 🌍 Declaration of Global Income
When executing your annual Japanese tax filings, you must assume that all global support funds, international bank transfers, and overseas donations used to sustain your life and ministry in Sendai must be declared. If you believe a specific international tax treaty or exemption applies to your situation, verify this through certified professional tax counsel rather than guessing.

## 📊 Individual Financial Recordkeeping
To ensure smooth tax processing and simple visa renewal cycles, you must maintain highly organized personal financial archives. Retain systematic records of all monthly support statements, international bank transfers, local ATM transactions, housing rental receipts, utility bills, and official municipal correspondence.

## 🚨 Delayed Compliance Mitigation
If you fall behind on your national health insurance payments, neglect your pension notices, or miscalculate your local resident tax obligations, take immediate corrective action. Contact our base administration officer to gather missing data or seek external professional legal advice. Delays heavily jeopardize your legal standing and visa eligibility in Japan.
