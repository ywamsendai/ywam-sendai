## 🏛️ Post-Arrival Administrative Checklist
Arriving long-term in Sendai triggers a mandatory sequence of legal and practical setup tasks. While your local YWAM community will assist you with paperwork, you are expected to proactively manage these steps to establish your legal and operational footprint in Japan.

## 🪪 Resident Registration & The Visa Rule
Within 14 days of moving into your initial accommodation, you must visit your local municipal ward office (区役所 - *Kuyakusho*) to register your address. Bring your passport and Residence Card (*Zairyū Card*). The staff will print your registered address on the back of your card. **Law:** You must carry your Residence Card physically on your person at all times.

## 🏥 National Health Insurance Registration
During your local ward office visit, you are legally required to register for National Health Insurance (*Kokumin Kenkō Hoken*). Health insurance requires a monthly premium based on income and covers 70% of all medical costs. You will receive a physical health insurance card; carry this with you at all times for medical appointments.

## 👵 National Pension System Compliance
You must also enroll in the National Pension System (*Kokumin Nenkin*) framework at the ward office. These civic systems are legally mandatory for all foreign residents inside Japan. Keep all official municipal pension notices and payment slips organized in your personal files.

## 🆔 Individual Number System (MyNumber)
You will receive an official notification containing your unique Individual Number profile, commonly known as "MyNumber". While applying for the physical plastic card is voluntary, it serves as a highly efficient, unified digital ID card. 

### 🛑 MyNumber Card Expiration Check
Your physical MyNumber card is tied directly to your visa expiration date. You must proactively renew this card at city hall every single time your visa is extended by immigration.

## 🪵 Customizing Your Stamp (Hanko/Inkan)
In Japan, a personalized ink stamp called a *Hanko* or *Inkan* engraved with your surname serves as your legal signature. You will use this stamp with red ink pads to validate bank accounts, lease agreements, and official base paperwork. Base staff will direct you to a local shop to create an inexpensive custom seal upon arrival.

## 🏦 Opening a Local Bank Account
Securing a Japanese bank account is an absolute necessity to manage your ministry funds, execute domestic wire transfers, and clear recurring utility invoices. You will set this account up immediately after registering your initial address and securing your *Hanko* stamp.

## 📱 Mobile Device Compatibility
To maintain community communication via Discord and LINE, you must possess an operational smartphone. If bringing a phone from home, ensure it is completely carrier-unlocked and compatible with GSM network bands before landing. Alternatively, you can purchase an unlocked SIM-free smartphone online or at local second-hand shops in Japan.

## 🌐 SIM Cards & Mobile Wallets
Acquire a local data-and-voice SIM plan online or via budget providers. Domestic calls carry per-minute fees, so prioritize data for app-to-app calls. If your phone supports *Saifu-Keitai* (Mobile Wallet circuitry), you can link it directly to transit apps and the MyNumber digital platform.

## 🏠 The Senzan Line Housing Mandate
YWAM Sendai does not maintain centralized staff housing; all members procure their own apartments. To preserve deep community proximity and ease of gathering, we strongly request that all staff members find housing within a short walking distance of a train station situated along the **Senzan Line**. 

## 📑 Leasing Real Estate in Japan
With base staff assistance, you will coordinate with a local real estate agency to locate an apartment matching your budget. Note that Japanese apartments are leased completely unfurnished, without appliances or internet hardware. Contracts usually lock for 2 years and require upfront initial fees covering agency administrative costs, insurance, and a security deposit.

## 🔄 Post-Move Address Updates
Once you receive the keys to your long-term apartment, you must repeat your administrative loop. Return to the municipal ward office within 14 days to officially update the address on your Residence Card. You must also immediately log this address change with your bank, mobile network provider, and the base leadership team.
