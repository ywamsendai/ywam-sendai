## 📜 Historical Reference Points
Over our history, the global YWAM movement has been intentionally shaped by specific spiritual words, corporate visions, and solemn covenant documents. These historical markers trace our shared identity, tracking seasons of course correction, alignment, and movement renewal. They do not replace local Sendai policy, but they define our shared vocabulary.

## 🌊 Core Legacy Words

### The Vision of the Waves (1956)
The initial foundational vision received by Loren Cunningham depicting waves of young people covering the continents. This historic word drives YWAM's long-standing emphasis on mobilizing youth for global missionary deployment.

### The Spheres of Influence (1975)
The strategic framework declaring that Christian witness must actively engage and serve the seven primary pillars of culture: **Family, Economy, Government, Religion, Education, Media, and Arts & Celebration**.

### The Christian Magna Carta (1981)
A milestone document declaring that every individual on Earth has a basic human right to hear the Gospel, access the Bible, join Christian fellowship, obtain education, and have access to basic physical necessities.

### Ending Bible Poverty
A modern, binding movement-wide commitment to partner with the wider body of Christ to ensure every language group on the planet has direct access to the Scriptures in their native tongue.

## 🤝 Historical Movement Covenants
* **Manila Covenant (1988):** Affirmed the multi-ministry scope of the mission (Evangelism, Training, Mercy) and established international diversity baselines.
* **Red Sea Covenant (1992):** A corporate call to intentional repentance and increased focus on the unreached fields of the Muslim world, resisting organizational pride.
* **Nanning Covenant (2002):** Re-anchored the movement in apostolic pioneering patterns, relationship-oriented leadership, and spiritual eldership over corporate management styles.
* **Jubilee Covenant (2010):** A movement-wide personal recommitment during YWAM's 50th anniversary to choose self-denial and execute the Great Commission.
* **Singapore Covenant (2014):** Explicitly protected our spiritual decentralization and freedom to hear God’s voice from restrictive, overly bureaucratic administrative systems.
