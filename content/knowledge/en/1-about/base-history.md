## ⏳ 2010–2018: Vision & Prayer
Before a permanent base manifested, YWAM Sendai was birthed through years of dedicated prayer walks, vision trips, and temporary outreach deployments. Gary and Naoko Seddon, serving out of YWAM Tokyo, led these consistent regional treks. Over nearly a decade, Sendai became crystal clear as our strategic, long-term ministry home.

## 🚀 2018: The Pioneering Launch
In summer 2018, Gary and Naoko Seddon, along with an additional pioneering couple, officially launched from YWAM Tokyo to plant YWAM Sendai. This foundational season deliberately bypassed rapid numerical expansion to focus strictly on building local neighborhood roots, setting house prayer rhythms, and learning to live faithfully in the city.

## 😷 2020–2023: Hidden Preparation
The global COVID-19 pandemic structurally confined our community to a prolonged season of waiting and interior focus. Outward public operations were heavily restricted, but this window became a vital catalyst for deep internal base strengthening, intensive Japanese language study, local cultural adaptation, and clarifying our unique community identity.

## 🌱 2023 Onward: Training & Growth
Our season of hidden preparation catalyzed a new era of visible mobilization and training multiplication. YWAM Sendai hit a massive corporate milestone by successfully launching our first fully bilingual Discipleship Training School (DTS) in September 2023, followed immediately by a second running intake in September 2024.

## 🪵 Staying True to the Roots
Our history directly dictates our current operational priorities. We refuse to chase hurried, corporate expansion. We look ahead to launching further training tracks—including the upcoming Discipleship Bible School (DBS)—while remaining fiercely committed to our baseline DNA: being deeply rooted in prayer, anchored in the local language, and entirely present in the place we call home.
