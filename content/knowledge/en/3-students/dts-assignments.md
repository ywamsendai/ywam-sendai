## 📅 Journal Submission Rules
To successfully track through the DTS, you must submit a personal processing journal every single week. 
* **The Deadline:** Every Monday morning before 08:30. 
* **Digital Entries:** Email directly to the appointed staff member.
* **Physical Entries:** Hand handwritten journals directly to your assigned staff mentor.

## 🗺️ Journal: Outreach Logs
Every week you will participate in two local ministries. You must log these experiences weekly by answering: What did you do? What was your strategy? What did God speak? How did the people respond? Note one specific area of personal growth.

## 🩸 Journal: Theological Study
Select one specific characteristic of God each week (e.g., grace, faithfulness, justice). Use a minimum of 5 distinct biblical scriptures to explain this trait. Write how this trait challenges your current lifestyle, worldview, or beliefs.

## ⛪ Journal: Reflection & Church
List three distinct things God spoke to you during the week across lectures, relationships, or quiet times, alongside practical application steps. Write a short paragraph summarizing your Sunday local church experience.

## 📚 Required Book Portfolio
During the lecture phase, read a minimum of 3 books and submit formal reports. Your core portfolio consists of:
1. *Is That Really You, God?* by Loren Cunningham.
2. *Making Jesus Lord* by Loren Cunningham.
3. A third missional or theological book of your own choice.

## 🎭 Creative Book Presentations
Your evaluation for *Is That Really You, God?* occurs via a live process group presentation (music, drama, video, or slides). Your presentation must isolate one principle of hearing God's voice, show personal application, and map a YWAM value to the text.

## 📊 The Tohoku Intercession Project
Every Monday afternoon, research an assigned prefecture in the Tohoku region to build long-term intercession tools. This research culminates in a mandatory 2-page A4 Prayer Resource submitted by the late-lecture deadline.

## 📈 Tohoku Project Data Points
Your 2-page A4 Prayer Resource must include: a map highlighting your chosen prefecture, its capital and major urban centers, total population, age demographics, estimated Christian presence, gospel barriers, and short/long-term ministry opportunities.
