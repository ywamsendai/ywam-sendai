## 🆘 Immediate Emergency Action
In any crisis situation, prioritize immediate, real-time physical safety over internal organizational hierarchy.
* **Dial 119:** Call this number immediately for all fire emergencies, ambulance needs, and medical crises.
* **Dial 110:** Call this number immediately for police intervention, accidents, or active security threats.
* **Notify Leadership:** Contact a YWAM Sendai leader as soon as your immediate physical safety is entirely secured.

## 📱 Digital Early-Warning Apps
Japan's automated disaster warning systems are incredibly advanced. You must install a disaster early-warning app on your smartphone immediately upon arrival to receive real-time earthquake and typhoon alerts:
* **For English Speakers:** Install **Yurekuru Call** (highly recommended for real-time seismic countdowns).
* **For Japanese Speakers/Learners:** Install **Yahoo! Japan防災速報** (*Bōsai Sokuhō*).

## 🎒 The 3-Day Grab-and-Go Bag
External disaster relief can take up to 72 hours to organize. Every community member should assemble or purchase a ready-made emergency backpack designed to sustain one adult for **1 to 3 days**. Ensure this bag is stored near your exit door for immediate access.
* **Water & Food:** long-life water (the most critical item), alpha rice, canned food, energy bars, and dried bread.
* **Valuables:** Physical cash, your passport, a copy of your Residence Card, and your health insurance card.

## 🧻 Emergency Bag Sanitation & Tools
In addition to food, your 3-day emergency backpack must contain tools and hygiene gear to handle infrastructural failure before external teams arrive:
* **Sanitation:** 4 to 21 simple portable toilets (災害用トイレ), toilet paper, wet tissues, first-aid kit, and sanitary items.
* **Gear:** A waterproof flashlight, a portable radio, a mobile phone charger/battery bank, a whistle, and a raincoat.
* **Infant Care:** Families with infants must pre-pack formula milk, baby bottles, and disposable nappies.

## 🏠 Home Shelter & 1-Week Emergency Stock
Statistically, **90% of people continue to live in their homes** following a natural disaster, as municipal evacuation centers prioritize those whose residences are structurally destroyed. Because local utilities (water, gas, electricity) may be completely cut off, you must maintain a **1-week supply stack** at your residence to extend your grab-and-go stock:
* **Extra Water:** 9L to 15L of additional long-life water per adult.
* **Extra Sanitation:** 24 to 28 additional simple portable toilets.
* **Food Reserves:** Extra shelf-stable cans, biscuits, and alpha rice.

## 🛏️ Earthquakes: Room Configuration & Evacuation
Preventing structural injury within your living space is your primary line of defense against seismic activity:
* **Furniture Anchoring:** Secure tall furniture, such as bookshelves or wardrobes, to walls or ceilings using extendable tension rods (つっぱり棒) or safety brackets.
* **Sleeping Zones:** Absolutely **never** place breakable or heavy items (photo frames, heavy clocks, mugs) on high shelves above or near your bed where they could fall during sleep.
* **Evacuation Paths:** Memorize the exact physical route to your neighborhood's designated municipal evacuation center (避難所 - *Hinanjo*). During a major quake: drop, cover, hold on, and exit the building only once the shaking stops.

## 🐻 Wildlife Awareness & Asian Black Bear Safety
The natural geography of Tohoku means wildlife occasionally moves into quieter, forested, or hilly residential zones near the city edges.
* **Be Hyper-Vigilant:** Exercise increased caution at dawn, dusk, and night when walking near wooded areas, parks, or remote riversides.
* **Make Noise:** When walking in quiet or wooded zones, make your presence known (e.g., talk or use a small bell) to avoid startling a bear.
* **Retreat Safely:** If you encounter a bear, **do not run, do not make eye contact, and do not stop to take photos.** Back away slowly, facing the animal calmly, and retreat into a secure structure. Report sightings immediately.

## 🛠️ Proactive Hazard Reporting
Do not wait for an accident to happen before fixing a problem. Report structural issues, water leaks, exposed wiring, or unsafe building conditions to the maintenance team the moment you spot them. Proactive hazard mapping keeps our entire community safe.
