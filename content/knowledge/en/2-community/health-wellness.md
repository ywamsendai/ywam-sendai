## 🍎 Taking Ownership of Your Health
Physical health is a vital shared asset. Your daily energy levels, emotional resilience, and capacity to serve others are directly linked to your physical habits. YWAM Sendai provides a supportive community environment, but the primary responsibility for health—including proper hydration, regular sleep patterns, physical movement, and balanced nutrition—rests entirely with you.

## 🥣 Dietary Allergies & Medical Disclosures
If you manage specific food allergies, strict dietary restrictions, or complex medical conditions, you must communicate these clearly to leadership before your arrival. This allows us to assess if we can safely integrate your needs into our shared housing and communal kitchen logistics. Clear, upfront communication is a baseline requirement for communal safety.

---

## 🛡️ Managing Illness & Medical Support

If you fall ill or experience a significant drop in physical well-being, communicate your status to your direct team leader immediately. Do not attempt to "push through" or hide health risks out of a false sense of duty. 

* **Community, Not Clinic:** YWAM Sendai is a missional community, not a healthcare provider or a medical clinic. 
* **Personal Responsibility:** While we offer local logistical support and prayer, you are fully responsible for seeking, scheduling, and funding professional medical consultations, prescriptions, and treatments. 
* **Insurance Baseline:** You must ensure your health insurance coverage is active, valid in Japan, and accessible immediately upon arrival.

---

## 🛌 Rest, Sustainability & Sabbath Limits
YWAM Sendai values long-term structural endurance and healthy physical boundaries over frantic ministry output. To protect your long-term health and prevent burnout:
* **Sabbath Honor:** All staff, students, and short-term visitors must protect their weekly Sabbath rest rhythms—unplugging from operational tasks to rest and recharge.
* **Planned Time-Off:** Extended time-off blocks, leave, or travel must be planned, coordinated, and logged in advance with leadership to ensure sustainable base tracking.

## 📵 Technology Use & Digital Boundaries

Technology is a tool designed to support our mission, not a master to fragment our focus. We practice clear digital boundaries to protect our mental health, cultivate deep spiritual focus, and remain fully present in face-to-face community life.

### 🕒 Communication & Sleep Guardrails
To protect shared living environments and maximize sleep quality, all community members adhere to these standard boundaries:
* **The 21:00 Messaging Cutoff:** Non-urgent team messaging, group chats, and operational platform activity must cease after 21:00. Emergency notifications are the only exception.
* **Pre-Sleep Unplugging:** We strongly recommend turning off all screens at least 60 minutes before your planned bedtime to ensure effective physical and neurological recovery.
* **Quiet Hours:** Strict quiet protocols apply across all shared housing units from 22:00 to 07:00.
* **Device-Free Zones:** Devices must be completely silenced and put away during worship, corporate prayer tracks, formal lectures, 1-on-1 mentoring sessions, and shared community meals.

### ⚖️ Deconstructing Constant Availability
True presence is far more important than instantaneous digital responsiveness. You are not required or expected to live in a state of constant, anxious reachability. Healthy community members check and reply to notifications responsibly at scheduled intervals throughout the day rather than reacting to every single buzz. Cultivating excessive digital urgency creates artificial, unhealthy pressure on the entire team.

---

## 📱 Social Media Integrity & Privacy

Your digital footprint directly impacts the public reputation and Gospel witness of YWAM Sendai. Use social media platforms with high wisdom and personal integrity:
* **No Public Venting:** Never use personal social media accounts to vent community frustrations, broadcast internal friction, or process relational conflicts. Keep those conversations face-to-face.
* **Explicit Consent First:** Always ask for explicit permission before filming or posting photos/videos of community members, staff children, or local Japanese friends. Respecting local privacy boundaries is non-negotiable.
* **Proactive Check-ins:** If digital habits or screen time start disrupting your sleep, work performance, or real-life community participation, expect a direct leadership check-in for proactive, supportive accountability.
