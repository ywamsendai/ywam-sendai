## 🚪 The Student Application Pipeline
YWAM Sendai processes all student applications through a centralized digital framework. Enrolling in a Discipleship Training School (DTS) or Disciple Makers Bible School (DBS) is an intentional process of spiritual alignment and relational discernment.

## 🔞 Student Entry Prerequisites
To be eligible for an upcoming school intake, you must clear these standard operational baselines before your file can be processed:
* **Minimum Age:** You must be at least 18 years old by your scheduled graduation date.
* **DTS Prerequisite:** You must possess a UofN verified DTS graduation certificate to apply for the DBS track.
* **Language Baseline:** You must have independent functional proficiency in either English or Japanese.

## 🛑 Student Visa Waiver Policy
YWAM Sendai provides **zero corporate visa sponsorship, legal guarantees, or official invitation letters** for school students. You are entirely responsible for tracking current Japanese border rules, verifying your passport validity, and entering Japan under a standard, automatic temporary visitor visa waiver.

## 📥 Student Onboarding: Steps 1 to 3
* **Step 1: Application Submittal:** Submit the master form online and select the student track.
* **Step 2: Reference Evaluation:** Our intake team contacts your pastors and peers to evaluate character and community readiness.
* **Step 3: Discernment Interview:** Participate in a video interview to discuss your calling, expectations, and processing timelines.

## ✉️ Student Onboarding: Steps 4 to 7
* **Step 4: Acceptance Notification:** If alignment is clear, you will receive an official digital acceptance letter.
* **Step 5: Tuition Fees:** Coordinate with the finance office to clear your necessary school fee payments.
* **Step 6: Border Check:** Confirm your passport and waiver logistics are secure. Do not book travel until accepted.
* **Step 7: On-Site Arrival:** Land in Sendai, check into base housing, and begin school orientation.

## 📦 Student Mobile Packing Limits
Students must pack lightly to remain fully mobile during lectures and outreach phases. You are strictly limited to two bags total:
* One primary piece of luggage (either a sturdy hiking backpack or a medium rolling suitcase).
* One small daypack for daily classroom use.

## 🎒 Student Gear Checklist
Your personal kit must prioritize space-saving items that fit into shared, high-density housing setups:
* Modest clothing suitable for seasonal Japanese weather and local church ministries.
* Personal toiletries, daily essentials, and specialized medical prescriptions.
* A durable, reusable personal water bottle.

## 📚 Student Academic Kit
Ensure your luggage contains these mandatory items for daily study and outreach tracking:
* A physical Bible and durable study stationery.
* A foldable umbrella and a reusable travel mug.
* A compact sleeping bag (absolutely non-negotiable for outreach tracking).
* Small hospitality gifts from your home country for local ministry hosts.

## 📧 Student Pre-Arrival Messaging
You must send consistent data updates to our team leading up to your school start date. Proactively flag any sudden travel changes. Your messages must explicitly confirm your exact arrival time, incoming flight numbers, and critical dietary or medical needs.

## 👾 Student Discord Mandate
All pre-arrival updates, logistical coordination, and last-minute airport pickup details are managed via our server. You must join and check the Discord server regularly. Audit the channels within 24 hours of landing in Japan for emergency updates.
