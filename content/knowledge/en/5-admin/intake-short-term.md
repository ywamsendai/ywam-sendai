## 🚪 Short-Term Guest & Team Pipeline
Short-term outreach teams, ministry exposure groups, and individual base visitors must log their stay requests through our digital intake portal. Short-term tracking is heavily dependent on seasonal base capacity and scheduling.

## 🛑 Strict Zero-Visa Sponsorship Rule
YWAM Sendai provides **zero corporate visa sponsorship, legal guarantees, or official invitation letters** for short-term teams or visitors. All individuals are completely responsible for managing their own passport validity and entering Japan under a standard, automatic temporary visitor visa waiver.

## 📋 Team Leader Responsibilities
Visiting team leaders retain complete primary oversight, behavioral accountability, and pastoral care for their members. Leaders must actively model base expectations, monitor team unity, resolve internal group tensions early, and execute all schedules in direct submission to local YWAM Sendai authority.

## 🗺️ Outreach Ministry Itineraries
Visiting outreach teams must not assume that a stay guarantees a customized ministry itinerary. If your group has specialized ministry goals, specific geographical hopes, or structural ideas for your outreach time, submit them to the base for evaluation prior to booking travel.

## 💴 Financial Readiness & Cash Rules
All short-term logistics, accommodation fees, and base operational charges must be settled before arrival. Because Japan’s regional infrastructure remains heavily cash-dependent, verify with base leadership which specific fees must be paid physically on-site using Japanese Yen cash currency.

## 📦 Short-Term Packing & Gear
Pack lightly to remain mobile and fit easily into high-density shared living environments. Your personal kit must prioritize:
* Modest, culturally respectful clothing appropriate for the current Japanese season.
* Personal toiletries, international travel insurance documents, and required prescription medications.
* A durable, reusable water bottle, a travel mug, and a compact umbrella.

## 📧 Pre-Arrival Coordination
Team leaders and guests must provide accurate data updates leading up to arrival. Flag any sudden changes immediately. Explicitly confirm exact arrival dates, total headcount changes (for groups), incoming flight numbers, and critical medical or dietary requirements.
