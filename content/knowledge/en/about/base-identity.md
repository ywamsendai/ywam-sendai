## 🏔️ Core Identity
YWAM Sendai is a Christian missional community rooted in Sendai, Japan. We operate as a localized expression of Youth With A Mission (YWAM), a global, decentralized, interdenominational movement tracking across cultures to follow Jesus and serve others. Our local blueprint is simple: love God, love people, and serve the unique realities of the Tohoku region.

## 🎯 Base Mission
Our operational focus is to know God and make Him known through rooted presence, discipleship, hospitality, and practical service. We purposefully choose to skip temporary, hype-driven events in favor of a sustainable, long-term witness. This looks like intentional, day-to-day rhythms: corporate worship, local neighborhood outreach, serious language acquisition, mercy ministries, and shared family-style meals.

## 📜 Theological Foundations
Our mission stands firmly on historic Christian orthodoxy and shared movement anchors:
* **Orthodoxy:** We hold to the absolute authority of Scripture and the historic confessions of the Church, including the Nicene Creed.
* **The Posture:** We maintain an intentional interdenominational stance. We do not build our own kingdom; we humbly support, cooperate with, and seek to strengthen the existing Japanese church network.

## 🕊️ Spiritual Vision Markers
We are here to participate in the spiritual and social renewal of northeastern Japan. We prioritize real, lasting fruit over shallow numerical metrics:
* **Encounter:** People coming into a firsthand relationship with Jesus Christ.
* **Formation:** Disciples formed in interior character, emotional health, and clear calling.
* **Unity:** Local churches relationally strengthened and validated, not replaced.
* **Presence:** A sustained Christian witness rooted in prayer, deep humility, and local love.

## 🗺️ The Tohoku Mandate Geography
The Tohoku mandate is our specific geographical calling to northeastern Japan's main island. This mandate encompasses six distinct prefectures: **Aomori, Iwate, Miyagi, Akita, Yamagata, and Fukushima**. We refuse to treat Sendai as an isolated bubble or a convenient base camp; it is our strategic springboard to prayerfully invest in this entire region over decades.

## 🎯 Why Focus Regionally?
Mission is most fruitful when it is localized, tangible, and accountable to real people and specific places. 
* **Real Ties:** A regional focus forces us to build authentic relationships, learn local histories, and avoid event-driven superficiality.
* **Collaboration:** We partner consistently across prefectural lines, taking responsibility for the parts of Japan that are easily overlooked.

## 🤝 The Role of Sendai
Sendai is the largest city in Tohoku and acts as our central operational hub. We steward this location to coordinate regional intercession, deploy teams beyond urban transit lines, host cross-cultural hospitality, and run immersive training tracks like DTS and DBS. We view our city presence not as an end in itself, but as a gateway to serve the underserved across the region.

## 🪵 Defining Long-Term Presence
Long-term presence means choosing faithfulness over speed, and relational depth over constant busyness. We intentionally opt out of standard corporate organizational pressure. We show up consistently by learning the Japanese language and culture seriously, honoring sustainable rhythms of work and rest, and slowly earning credibility and trust within our local neighborhood.

## 🎓 Focus on Training
We emphasize training because effective missions require healthy, resilient people. Through schools like DTS and DBS, intentional mentoring, and shared community life, we develop practical ministry readiness. We focus deeply on cultivating inner spiritual intimacy, biblical understanding, and high cultural humility before sending workers out.
