## 🛠️ Non-Employment Ministry Model
Staff service at YWAM Sendai operates within a voluntary, missionary-community framework rather than a corporate employment model. Long-term staff are self-supported individuals who share active spiritual responsibility, demonstrate deep flexibility, and willingly adapt to meet the pioneering operational needs of the Tohoku region.

## 📊 The 30+5 Weekly Rhythm
To maintain functional health and operational clarity, the base implements a mandatory **30+5 weekly hourly tracking baseline**:
* **30 Hours of Core Ministry & Operations:** Includes running UofN training schools, mentoring, base maintenance, local outreach tracking, and hospitality care.
* **5 Hours of Focused Personal Development:** Reserved strictly for Japanese language acquisition, theological reading, and specific ministry skill development.

## 🗓️ Mandatory Core Alignment
Presence in our shared communal spaces is required. Staff must treat community rhythms as core duties, not optional add-ons. This requires punctual attendance at all base prayer and worship gatherings, shared meals, and our weekly **Core Alignment meeting every Monday**. This session is our vital operational hub for tracking base logistics, financial status updates, and strategic planning.

## 📅 Standard Leave Parameters
Extended time-off allotments are categorized across three distinct operational tracking blocks. Staff must not postpone these rhythms indefinitely in a manner that damages emotional or physical health:

| Leave Type | Duration | Tracking Frequency | Primary Operational Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Leave** | 10 Days | Every Calendar Year | Full mental disconnection from normal base responsibilities. |
| **Furlough** | 3 Weeks | Every Calendar Year | Home-country relationship care and donor network development. |
| **Sabbatical** | 3 Months | Every 5 to 7 Years | Deep physical recovery, theological study, and spiritual renewal. |

## 💴 Shared Base Community Contribution
As part of our commitment to shared administrative stewardship and the upkeep of our communal facilities, all active long-term staff members pay a fixed monthly base fee. This baseline is currently set at **¥6,000 per month**. Follow our standard financial tracking workflow to clear this contribution on time.

## 👥 Mentoring & Confidentiality
Staff are expected to actively cultivate a deep culture of discipleship by participating in weekly or bi-weekly 1-on-1 pastoral mentoring conversations. Handle all personal information shared during these sessions with high maturity. Maintain absolute privacy unless a concern directly involves physical safety, safeguarding violations, or structural leadership intervention.

## ⚓ Spiritual Posture & Integrity
Active ministry presence must always flow out of an authentic, interior life with God. Staff members are expected to cultivate consistent personal habits of prayer, Scripture study, worship, and teachability. Your public representation of the mission must match your private conduct, financial dealings, and interpersonal relationships with flawless integrity.

## 🏔️ Pioneering Posture
YWAM Sendai operates within a pioneering, community-driven missional environment. We look for team members who are spiritually grounded, teachable, and flexible. You must be prepared to contribute to ordinary, practical base chores and administrative tasks alongside public spiritual ministries. Success requires long-term commitment over short-term excitement.

## 🗣️ Linguistic Growth Commitment
Staff applicants do not need advanced Japanese fluency to apply, but you must demonstrate an active commitment to language acquisition. Long-term staff are expected to participate in base language tracks and dedicate personal energy toward cultural adaptation. Sincere presence in Sendai requires a posture of listening, learning, and local relational integration.

## 💼 Total Self-Support Mandate
All long-term missionary staff members at YWAM Sendai operate as self-supported volunteers. The base does not provide salaries, wages, or stipends. Accepted applicants must actively build a personal support team of churches, families, and partners to secure reliable monthly funding before relocating to Sendai.

## 💴 Monthly Financial Benchmarks
To protect team members from severe financial stress, YWAM Sendai enforces realistic monthly fundraising baselines. These ranges reflect the true cost of housing, food, civic compliance fees, and local transport inside Japan. You must prove your personal support network meets these minimum parameters before arrival tracking can be activated:
* **Single Staff:** ¥140,000 to ¥170,000 per month.
* **Married Couple:** ¥240,000 to ¥280,000 per month.
* **Family with Children:** ¥320,000 to ¥450,000+ per month.

## 🤝 Relational Partnership Logic
Fundraising is an ongoing relational partnership, not a series of transactional appeals. Staff must proactively invite churches, families, and friends into their missional calling. Build your sending base through personal video calls, face-to-face meetings, and clear presentations rather than relying passively on generic written materials.

## 📧 Consistent Partner Updates
Maintain active, high-integrity communication with your supporter network through consistent digital updates, prayer requests, and expressions of gratitude. Do not limit your communication to seasons when your bank balance is low. Healthy communication builds long-term sustainability and honors the deep trust your financial partners place in your ministry.

## 🚨 Early Financial Alerts
If your monthly support levels weaken or fall below our mandatory base benchmarks, you must notify the finance office immediately. Do not hide funding shortfalls. Our team will work with you early to review your budget, adjust your operational timelines, or deploy targeted partnership development strategies before the shortfall escalates into a lifestyle crisis.
